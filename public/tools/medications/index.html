Medication Questions

These are the questions that come up a dozen times a shift. “Can I give these two together?” “What’s the drip rate?” “Does this need a dose adjustment for their kidneys?” Just type the question and Lumen handles the rest.

Available Tools

💊
Drug Name Lookup (RxNorm)
Resolves drug names via NLM RxNorm. Works with brand names, generics, abbreviations, and even misspellings. Returns the normalized name, RxCUI, ingredients, and drug class.
"Look up the generic name for Jardiance"
Lookup
⚠
Drug Interactions
Checks drug-drug interactions using the FDA interaction database. Returns severity level, mechanism of interaction, and clinical management recommendations.
"Check interactions between warfarin, amiodarone, and metoprolol"
Safety
💧
IV Drip Calculator
Calculates IV infusion rates from weight-based dosing. Computes mL/hr with titration guidance. Supports common drips like vasopressors, insulin, and heparin.
"Calculate norepinephrine drip at 0.1 mcg/kg/min for an 80kg patient"
Calculator
🩸
Renal Dose Adjustment
Computes creatinine clearance (Cockcroft-Gault), determines CKD stage, and looks up FDA-recommended dose adjustments for renal impairment.
"Renal dose check for vancomycin in a 70-year-old male, 85kg, SCr 2.1"
Calculator
📈
FDA Adverse Events
Searches the FDA FAERS (adverse event reporting) database. Aggregates reaction reports by drug, showing frequencies, outcomes, and severity distributions.
"What are the most reported adverse events for methotrexate?"
Safety
🚨
FDA Drug Recalls
Checks the FDA enforcement and recall database. Shows recall classifications, affected lots, and current status.
"Are there any recent recalls for metformin products?"
Safety

Renal Dosing Workflow

The renal dose tool needs specific patient parameters. Lumen will ask for anything you don’t provide upfront:

Parameter What It Is Example
Drug The medication to adjust Vancomycin
Age Patient age in years 70
Sex Biological sex (for CrCl) Male
Weight Body weight in kg 85
SCr Serum creatinine in mg/dL 2.1
💡 Tip
Provide all parameters upfront
The more information you include in your initial message, the faster you’ll get results. For example: “Renal dose check for gabapentin: 65yo female, 60kg, SCr 1.8”
❗
For clinical use, always verify drug interaction and dosing information with your institution’s pharmacy resources. Lumen is a decision support tool, not a replacement for clinical judgment.
