Where It Gets Its Info

Lumen always shows its work. After every answer, you’ll see a “Sources” link that tells you exactly which guidelines, studies, or databases the answer came from — and how closely each one matched your question. Think of it as the bibliography at the end of a paper.

The Sources Chip

After a response that draws on evidence, you’ll see a sources chip:

📚 Sources (4)

Click this chip to expand the list of sources inline, or click “View in panel” to open them in the side panel for a detailed view.

Understanding Similarity Scores

Each source has a match percentage that indicates how closely it relates to your question:

80–100% — Highly Relevant
Strong match. This source directly addresses your question. Shown in green.
60–79% — Relevant
Good match. This source covers related topics. Shown in gold.
Below 60% — Supporting
Partial match. Provides background context. Shown in muted gray.

Source Types

Depending on which knowledge toggles you have enabled, sources can come from:

  • Clinical Database — Peer-reviewed guidelines, drug databases, clinical protocols
  • My Docs — Your personally uploaded documents and notes
  • Web — Live internet search results with URLs
💡
Sources aren’t just decorative. They’re the evidence chain for Lumen’s answer. If a response doesn’t have the sources you’d expect, try enabling additional knowledge toggles or rephrasing your question.
